# Reference Timeline

This file is the human-readable historical anchor for the sandbox.

## 1939

### September-December 1939

- September 1: Germany invades Poland with 1.5 million troops. The campaign lasts 35 days. Polish military dead: ~66,000; POWs: ~694,000 (MGFA). **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- September 17: The USSR invades eastern Poland under the Molotov-Ribbentrop secret protocol, annexing ~200,000 km2 (Glantz).
- September 3: Britain and France declare war on Germany but take no offensive action. The "Phoney War" begins on the western front.
- German U-boats begin Atlantic operations. U-47 sinks HMS Royal Oak at Scapa Flow (October 14). Monthly shipping losses: ~150 kt GRT (Blair).
- German war economy is not fully mobilized. Armaments production constrained by competing priorities and institutional fragmentation (Tooze).

## 1940

### January-March 1940

- The Phoney War continues. Allied planning focuses on operations in Scandinavia and potential intervention in Finland (Winter War).
- German planning for Fall Gelb (western offensive) undergoes multiple revisions. Manstein's sickle-cut plan through the Ardennes is adopted (MGFA). **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- Soviet-Finnish Winter War ends March 13. Soviet performance is poor, reinforcing German and Western contempt for Red Army capability (Glantz).

### April-June 1940

- April 9: Germany invades Denmark and Norway. Denmark capitulates immediately. Norway falls by June despite Allied intervention.
- May 10: Germany launches Fall Gelb. Panzer breakthrough at Sedan (May 13) splits the Allied front.
- May 26 - June 4: Dunkirk evacuation rescues ~338,000 Allied troops but all heavy equipment is lost (Overy).
- June 10: Italy declares war on France and Britain (Knox).
- June 22: France signs armistice. Campaign duration: 6 weeks. French POWs: ~1.8 million. German dead: ~27,000 (MGFA). **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- Germany now dominates continental western Europe. The strategic question becomes whether and how to force Britain to terms.

### July-December 1940

- July-October: Battle of Britain. Luftwaffe loses ~1,733 aircraft vs RAF ~915 (Overy). Hitler postpones Sea Lion indefinitely. **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- September 7: The Blitz begins. London and other cities bombed through May 1941. ~43,000 British civilians killed (Overy).
- September 27: Tripartite Pact signed (Germany, Italy, Japan).
- October 28: Italy invades Greece and is immediately humiliated, requiring German intervention.
- December: British counter-offensive in North Africa destroys the Italian 10th Army (130,000 POWs).
- Hitler issues Directive 21 (December 18): planning for Barbarossa begins (Kershaw).
- German oil position: total supply ~8-9 Mt/year, consumption roughly matching. Synthetic fuel ~4 Mt/year, Romania ~2.8 Mt/year (Tooze).

## 1941

### January-March 1941

- February: Rommel arrives in North Africa with the Afrika Korps to rescue the Italian position.
- February: Fritz Todt heads the Armaments Ministry. German industrial mobilization remains incomplete and fragmented (Tooze).
- March 11: US Congress passes the Lend-Lease Act. Material support to Britain (and later USSR) formalizes (Harrison).
- British codebreaking at Bletchley Park reads Luftwaffe and Army Enigma with increasing reliability (Hinsley).

### April-June 1941

- April: Germany invades Yugoslavia and Greece. Both campaigns succeed within three weeks. Crete taken by costly airborne assault in May. The Balkans campaign may have delayed Barbarossa by several weeks. **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- Wehrmacht strength: ~7.23 million total (Mueller/MGFA).
- Barbarossa forces: ~3.3 million German troops in 148 divisions plus ~600,000 Axis allied troops (Glantz).
- German oil stockpile entering Barbarossa: ~950 kt-1.2 Mt available reserve. Enough for 2-3 months of intense operations (Tooze; USSBS).
- Einsatzgruppen deploy with the invasion force. Commissar Order and Barbarossa Decree authorize murder of Soviet political officials and suspend legal protections for Soviet civilians (Browning).
- June 22: Barbarossa launches. ~1,200 Soviet aircraft destroyed on the first day. **[DECISION WINDOW]**

### July-September 1941

- July-September: Battle of Smolensk. First serious Soviet resistance delays the advance for two months (Glantz). **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- Hitler diverts forces south from the Moscow axis to encircle Kiev. The Kiev pocket yields 665,000 Soviet POWs — the largest single encirclement in military history — but consumes weeks before autumn (Glantz; MGFA). **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- July-December: Soviet industrial evacuation moves ~1,523 enterprises and ~10 million workers east of the Urals. One of the most consequential logistical operations in history (Harrison).
- Einsatzgruppen mass shootings intensify. Babi Yar massacre: ~33,700 Jews killed September 29-30 (Browning; Hilberg).
- Supply status degrades rapidly as the advance outpaces rail conversion and truck endurance. By late summer, ~40% of trucks are out of service (Van Creveld; Stahel).
- German eastern front casualties by end of September: ~550,000 (Krivosheev; MGFA).

### October-December 1941

- October: Operation Typhoon (drive on Moscow) begins. Initial success; three more large encirclements.
- November: The autumn rasputitsa (mud season) halts movement. Supply becomes critical.
- December 5: Soviet counteroffensive before Moscow begins. Germans driven back 100-250 km in places (Glantz). **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- December 7: Pearl Harbor. US enters the war. Germany declares war on the US December 11.
- December 19: Hitler dismisses Brauchitsch and assumes direct command of the Army.
- German eastern front casualties to date: ~830,000 (MGFA). Truck pool reduced by ~40%. Fuel reserves severely drawn down.
- German fighter production: ~250/month. Soviet tank production: ~600/month and rising. UK aircraft production: ~1,650/month (Overy; Harrison).

## 1942

### January-March 1942

- January 20: Wannsee Conference coordinates administrative implementation of the Final Solution. Death camp construction accelerates (Browning; Hilberg).
- February 8: Fritz Todt dies. Albert Speer takes over the Armaments Ministry and begins rationalization reforms that will nearly triple output by 1944 (Tooze; Wagenfuhr).
- The eastern front stabilizes. Hitler's no-retreat order may have prevented a rout but also begins the pattern of refusing operational flexibility (Stahel).
- U-boat war enters its most destructive phase. After US entry, unprotected coastal shipping provides easy targets. Sinkings rise sharply (Blair).

### April-June 1942

- German armaments index begins climbing under Speer's reforms: 142 (1942) vs 100 (Jan-Feb 1942 baseline) (Wagenfuhr).
- June 28: Case Blue (Fall Blau) launches — the 1942 summer offensive toward the Caucasus oil fields and Stalingrad. Forces split into two diverging axes. **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- June 4-7: Battle of Midway. US Navy sinks four Japanese fleet carriers, shifting the Pacific naval balance.
- Atlantic: U-boat sinkings peak. Monthly losses reach ~550-700 kt GRT in mid-1942 (Blair; Roskill).
- Soviet industrial evacuation largely complete. Tank production recovers and exceeds 2,000/month by mid-1942 (Harrison).

### July-September 1942

- The Caucasus offensive fails to reach the Baku oil fields.
- 6th Army pushes into Stalingrad. Urban combat becomes a grinding attritional battle that devours German strength (Glantz).
- German logistics overextended; southern axis supply lines run through a single rail junction at Rostov.
- Soviet forces build up reserves for a massive counteroffensive north and south of Stalingrad.

### October-December 1942

- October 23 - November 11: Second El Alamein. Montgomery's 8th Army defeats Rommel. Axis threat to Egypt and Suez ends (Overy).
- November 8: Operation Torch — Anglo-American landings in French North Africa (Overy).
- November 19-23: Operation Uranus encircles the German 6th Army at Stalingrad. ~250,000 Axis troops trapped. Hitler forbids breakout. **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- Goering promises airlift of 300 t/day; actual delivery averages ~90 t/day. The pocket is doomed (Glantz).
- Death camps operating at peak capacity. Treblinka, Belzec, Sobibor, Auschwitz-Birkenau processing transports from across occupied Europe (Hilberg).
- U-boats operational: ~170 boats. Monthly Allied shipping losses: ~600-700 kt GRT (Blair).
- German fighter production rising: ~400/month (USSBS).

## 1943

### January-March 1943

- February 2: Field Marshal Paulus surrenders at Stalingrad. 91,000 POWs; fewer than 6,000 will return. Total Axis losses in the Stalingrad campaign: ~800,000 (Glantz; Krivosheev).
- February 18: Goebbels delivers the "total war" speech. Despite the rhetoric, German mobilization still falls short of British or Soviet levels (Tooze).
- Manstein's counterattack recaptures Kharkov (March), temporarily stabilizing the southern front.
- German armaments production continues rising under Speer. Tanks: ~550/month (Wagenfuhr).

### April-June 1943

- May 1943: U-boat losses spike to 41 boats (25% monthly loss rate). Doenitz withdraws from the North Atlantic. The Battle of the Atlantic turns decisively against Germany (Blair). Allied escort carriers, long-range aircraft, radar, and Ultra intelligence have proven decisive.
- May 13: Axis forces in Tunisia surrender. 230,000 POWs (Overy).
- German planning for Operation Citadel (Kursk offensive). Soviet intelligence knows the plan in detail (Glantz; Hinsley). **[DECISION WINDOW]**

### July-September 1943

- July 5-23: Battle of Kursk. The last major German offensive on the Eastern Front. German tanks committed: ~2,700; Soviet: ~5,000. Soviet defensive preparations absorb the German attack. Subsequent Soviet counteroffensives establish permanent westward momentum (Glantz; MGFA). **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- July 10: Allies invade Sicily.
- July 24-30: Operation Gomorrah. Hamburg firestorm kills ~37,000 civilians, destroys 50% of the city's buildings. Speer warns that six more such attacks could end the war (Overy; USSBS).
- September 8: Italy signs armistice. Germany immediately occupies northern Italy and disarms ~600,000 Italian troops. The Italian campaign becomes a grinding attritional theater (MGFA).
- August and October: Schweinfurt raids. Unescorted B-17s suffer catastrophic losses (26% on October 14). Deep penetration bombing without escort proven unsustainable (USSBS).

### October-December 1943

- November 28 - December 1: Tehran Conference. Roosevelt, Churchill, Stalin agree on Overlord for May 1944. Stalin commits to simultaneous eastern offensive (Overy).
- Soviet forces advance steadily westward. Kiev liberated November 6.
- German fighter production: ~550/month. German tank production: ~700/month. But Luftwaffe pilot quality declining under attrition (USSBS; Wagenfuhr).
- U-boat war continues but at reduced effectiveness. Monthly Allied losses: ~180 kt GRT (Blair).
- Allied strategic bombing intensifying but target selection remains debated. No systematic attack on fuel yet.

## 1944

### January-March 1944

- January 22: Anzio landings in Italy. The beachhead is contained and becomes a siege.
- February 20-25: Operation Argument ("Big Week"). ~6,000 bomber sorties target German aircraft factories. Physical damage repaired, but Luftwaffe fighter attrition accelerated by P-51 Mustang long-range escorts is decisive (Overy; USSBS).
- Speer's armaments output approaches its peak. Fighter production: ~1,600/month. Tank production: ~1,200/month (Wagenfuhr). But these numbers are increasingly meaningless without fuel.
- Soviet forces approach the pre-war Soviet western border.

### April-June 1944

- May 12: USAAF begins systematic attacks on German synthetic fuel plants. By September, aviation fuel output will fall from ~316 kt to ~17 kt per month — a 95% reduction. This is the single most decisive strategic bombing campaign of the war (USSBS; Tooze). **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- June 6: D-Day. ~156,000 Allied troops land in Normandy. All five beachheads secured despite fierce resistance. The western lodgment is established. **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- June 22: Operation Bagration destroys Army Group Center. ~400,000 German casualties; 28 divisions destroyed. The Soviets advance 600 km in two months, reaching the Vistula (Glantz; MGFA). **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- German fighter production peaks at ~2,200-2,500/month but aircraft cannot fly without fuel and trained pilots (USSBS).

### July-September 1944

- July 20: Assassination attempt against Hitler fails. Purge eliminates the conservative military resistance. ~200 executed, ~7,000 arrested. The regime radicalizes further (Kershaw). **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- August 23: Romania switches sides. Germany loses Ploesti oil (~2 Mt/year). The fuel crisis becomes terminal (Tooze). **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- August 25: Paris liberated.
- September 17-25: Operation Market Garden fails to secure a bridge at Arnhem.
- September: German synthetic fuel output at ~17 kt/month, down from ~316 kt in May. Pilot training hours collapse. Panzer formations immobilized (USSBS).
- Death marches from eastern concentration camps begin as the Soviet advance approaches (Hilberg).

### October-December 1944

- October: Hungary attempts to defect; Germany stages a coup (Operation Panzerfaust), installing Arrow Cross fascists.
- December 16: Hitler launches the Ardennes offensive with the last strategic reserves. ~100,000 German casualties; ~800 tanks lost; ~40 kt of irreplaceable fuel consumed. The offensive fails (Overy; MGFA). **[DECISION WINDOW]**
- Synthetic fuel output effectively zero by late December. Luftwaffe largely grounded. Wehrmacht mobility collapsing (USSBS).
- German fighter production still high (~2,000/month) but airframes without fuel are useless.
- Auschwitz liberated by Soviet forces January 27 1945. ~1.1 million murdered there in total (Hilberg).

## 1945

### January-May 1945

- January 12: Soviet Vistula-Oder offensive advances 500 km in three weeks, reaching the Oder River 60 km from Berlin (Glantz; Krivosheev).
- February 4-11: Yalta Conference divides post-war Europe.
- February 13-15: Bombing of Dresden kills ~25,000 (revised estimates; Overy).
- March 7: US forces cross the Rhine at Remagen.
- German industrial production collapses as the transportation network is destroyed by bombing. Speer's output figures become meaningless (Tooze; USSBS).
- April 16: Soviet Berlin offensive begins. ~2.5 million Soviet troops (Glantz).
- April 30: Hitler commits suicide in the Berlin bunker.
- May 8: Germany surrenders unconditionally.
- Total German military dead: ~5.3 million. Total Soviet military dead: ~8.7-11.4 million. Total European war dead: ~40 million including ~6 million Holocaust victims (Krivosheev; Overy; Hilberg).
